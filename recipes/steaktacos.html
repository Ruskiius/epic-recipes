How to Make Steak (Carne Asada) Tacos

By, Lalo Salamanca

Here's what you're making:

Carne Asada Tacos, you ever heard of them? Of course you have, everyone knows what they are. But let me tell you, not all Carne Asada Tacos are created equal. You gotta know how to make them right.

Carne Asada Tacos are made with grilled or pan-fried steak, usually skirt or flank steak, which is then thinly sliced and placed in a warm corn tortilla, sometimes with diced onions and cilantro. The marinade for the steak can vary, but typically includes lime juice, garlic, and chili powder for a little kick.

This is what you need:

  • 2lbs flank steak or skirt steak
  • 1/2 cup of orange juice
  • 1/2 cup of lime juice
  • 1/4 cup of olive oil
  • 4 cloves of minced garlic
  • 2 tsp of cumin
  • 1 tsp of chili powder (add extra for more zest
    • )
  • Salt and pepper
  • Corn or flower tortillas
  • Taco toppings of your choice (salsa, cilantro, onion, cheese, lime wedge, etc.)
  1. In a large resealable bag or a shallow dish, mix together the orange juice, lime juice, olive oil, garlic, cumin, oregano, chili powder, salt and pepper.
  2. Add the steak to the marinade and toss to coat. Marinate for at least 2 hours or up to overnight in the refrigerator. Lalo personally recommends overnight for maximum zest.
  3. Preheat grill to high heat
  4. Remove the steak from the marinade, shaking off any excess.
  5. Grill the steak for 3-4 minutes per side for medium-rare or until desired doneness.
  6. Remove from grill and let it rest for 5-10 minutes for slicing
  7. Heat the tortillas on the grill or in a dry skillet until it's warm and pliable.
  8. Serve the steak with some warm tortillas, toppings of your desire and enjoy amigo!
